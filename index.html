BORDER AS METHOD- SPRING 2021

How are borders recreated in online communities?



In a world where our lives have moved primarily or even entirely online, it becomes critical to examine the digital structures that are now facilitating our social lives. The internet has an extensive history both as a technology and as a platform for communication. It creates a space that supports a variety of subcultures and communities. Internet history is largely unexplored and undocumented, especially regarding its social usage. Looking at these communities at a micro level is definitely interesting and worthwhile, but in today’s circumstances it becomes more urgent to look at the phenomenon of online communities at a macro level. The problems of our real and in-person lives appear differently through online mediums, and it’s critical to examine the ways in which the regular borders of our lives are impacted by method and platform. First, I explore the phenomenon of the “virtual self”, or the personas that people adopt online. Then, I cover a brief history of the social applications of the internet, as well as my own experiences with online communities. Finally, I look to the phenomenon of borders, focusing on “real” borders that are recreated, as well as new and uniquely online borders that are presented.
